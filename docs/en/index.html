CHAP-V: A Comprehensive Carbon Reduction Platform

  • Rapid Deployment

    Current Practices

    • Vehicle data relies on OBD-II, which varies greatly among different vehicle models, resulting in lengthy initial setup.
    • OBD-II connections to vehicle computers carry the risk of vehicle malfunctions.

    CHAP-V

    • Instant use with in-vehicle installation; combined with GIS geographic information, it computes vehicle driving behavior in real-time.
    • Even without in-vehicle installation, CHAP-V can be deployed via mobile, facilitating agile fleet carbon management solutions.

  • High Adaptability

    Current Practices

    • Different vehicle models have different OBD-II data formats, often leading to data translation issues and data omissions.

    CHAP-V

    • Deployment is possible for various vehicle models without integrating OBD-II systems, allowing for vehicle behavior analysis.
    • If vehicles are already equipped with in-vehicle devices, existing data can be integrated and analyzed via APIs.

  • SaaS Cloud Platform

    Current Practices

    • Manual collection of paper fuel receipts and employee commuting data is complex, time-consuming, and error-prone.
    • Complete calculation evidence is required when third-party verification is needed.

    CHAP-V

    • Automated data collection processes; all vehicle behavior information is stored in the cloud platform with high-grade data security.
    • Automatic generation of fuel consumption and carbon emission calculation results, eliminating the possibility of manual calculation errors and enhancing verification credibility.

  • AI Smart Interpretation

    Current Practices

    • Even with collected vehicle driving behavior data, only generic statistical charts can be generated, requiring significant manual interpretation by managers.

    CHAP-V

    AI Interpretation Automatically Generates
    Carbon Reduction Strategies

    • AI interpretation provides carbon reduction optimization suggestions to managers, saving time on manual interpretation.
    • Up to 35% carbon reduction potential has been observed through actual testing.
